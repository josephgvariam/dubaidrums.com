Greetings Friends!

I haven't written a foreword for almost a year, because I couldnt find the right words to express how I was feeling.  I must admit, I didn't realize it would take me quite so long to process, so here goes:
I love to learn and be inspired.  I am constantly feeding my brain with new ideas from family, friends, books, internet, videos, TV, and personal growth courses.  But there is a big difference between using someone's ideas to get your creative juices flowing, and flat out stealing someone else's creativity.  They say that imitation is the greatest form of flattery and I believe that is true when there are ethics involved, or if it means revamping old ideas with a fresh perspective and giving credit where credit is due.  However, when it involves unethical behavior, downright plagiarism or allowing people to believe that you are the innovator when you are really the imitator, then it is so wrong and totally out of order.
 
Last year, I discovered that several people from Africa that I had either helped considerably in the past when they were out of work and then disassociated with because their values system left a lot to be desired; or employed and then terminated because they were working elsewhere behind my back, were leeching off of my hard work and success and riding on my reputation and well known brand.  They were ganging together (although they all work under different sponsors) and actively calling event companies to say that they would considerably undercut any prices of Dubai Drums in an attempt to put me out of business. How do I know this? - because event company owners, and others, that have dealt with me for the past 12 years and honor the relationship we have built, rang me to alert me.      
 
It shocked me and I started to ask myself questions like:  How can people do this?  How does the market allow them to get away with it?   Where have ethics and integrity gone?  Has it really become such a jungle out there?  Don't event companies care that when they pay less they generally get lower standards?  Have we really reached the stage where people prefer to put the value of making money at any cost before relationship, quality and professionalism?  
 
I would love for this kind of below the line energy to stop, not just to me, but to anyone that diligently works in a fair way and then gets cheated by scoundrels who want to take the easy, lazy way.   People that leech off of other people and ride on the coat-tails of their success know they are doing it; but figure its fine, and think they can get away with it because nobody calls them out on it.   In my case, quite often the imitators are not up to the standard of professionalism and expertise that is required for the kind of work that they are putting themselves forward for; and sometimes clients hire them because they believe they are associated with Dubai Drums.  When this happens, I end up apologizing for sub standard work that was not my own, or get called in to complete an event.  I believe that a lot of these people are operating out of fear and scarcity and "lack of abundance" mentality and with these kind of energies, unfortunately, end up polluting the waters of wherever they swim. 
 
Now that I have put the fear of 'speaking out' aside, and I am straightforwardly talking about this, its feels very freeing.  So here's what I want to say to the copycats and those that choose to deal with them:
 
Work ethically.  Stop riding off of other's success.  Create your own ideas.  If you want to steal ideas, be prepared to pay a franchise for them.  Understand that people work long and hard to get their companies to a well respected place and It's not okay for you to come along and think you can do it the easy, corrupt way.  When you copy without the skill required for the job you bring down the quality in the market and everyone suffers.  
 
The good news is - and what I relish in the fact of - that we must be really successful and famous because people want to copy what we do. So here's what I also want to say to the copycats:

Thank you for giving me a kick out of the nest and inspiring me to create better ideas.  As a result of your actions I have become more inventive, more innovative, more creative and more successful than ever before. 
 
They so rightly say:  "The things that challenge you the most in life is where you get the greatest learning".  Obviously, we need to take responsibility for the lessons we manifest in our world and then take the appropriate learning action.  I took a huge, painful, valuable lesson and learnt a lot from it!    My responsibility for my part in all of the above is that I was far too trusting, to the point of stupidity; I did not protect my interests well enough with the wrong kind of people, and I naively believed that I was being altruistic when I was actually totally gullible and allowing myself to be taken for a ride. 
 
As a certified relationship systems coach, I know that when a voice speaks, it is a voice of the system (living system) - so I am confident that others must be feeling the same way as I do about unethical behaviour.  I can see lack of integrity appearing on so many different levels and I want to put a call out to bring more ethics and values of decency, honesty, respect, collaboration and "healthy competition" into our world.  I hope my words spark others to take a stand and speak out against anything or anyone that is not enhancing their life.
 
I have found my voice.  My world feels great now.  Thank you for listening.
 

Rhythmic wishes to everyone
